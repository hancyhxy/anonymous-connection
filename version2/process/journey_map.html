USER JOURNEY MAP / Anonymous Social Connection

USER INFO

"I want to meet people — I just don't want to be the one who starts the conversation."

Mia, 23, UTS master's student, 6 months in Sydney. Loves meetups but usually stands by the drinks table scrolling her phone. Mild approach anxiety around strangers.

SCENARIO

Mia enters a social event where Hello Stranger is deployed. A collective screen sits in the room, and each attendee holds a small display device. She scans the QR code, sets up her anonymous avatar, picks interest topics, and then uses the screen + device to find someone worth approaching.

EXPECTATIONS

- Find someone with shared interests without cold-approaching
- Stay anonymous — no full name, no social handle
- Get a clear "confirmed" moment before speaking
- Receive a conversation starter, not just raw data

PROCESS ACTIONS
  • Scans QR code and opens the web page
  • Customizes avatar (shape / color / accessory)
  • Picks several interest topics
  • Submits; handheld device syncs personal content
  • Looks up at the collective screen
  • Watches avatars reorder, shift, and form relational lines
  • Guesses the logic behind the arrangement
  • Locates her own avatar within the group
  • Cross-references on-screen cues with her handheld display
  • Moves around the space
  • Observes avatars shown on others' devices
  • Narrows down to a single candidate
  • Approaches the target person
  • Initiates a tap (device proximity / touch)
  • Waits for synchronized feedback on both devices
  • Registers the confirmation
  • Reads the shared topics surfaced on her device
  • Picks one as an opener
  • Has a 3–5 minute face-to-face conversation
  • Exits naturally, ready for the next round
